45‑Minute Lesson: Upper‑Intermediate British English — Vocabulary & Pronunciation

Student profile: Upper‑intermediate (B2+). Goals: expand active vocabulary on abstract topics, reduce L1 accent interference in target sounds, improve stress and intonation for natural spoken British English.

Materials: short reading (provided), audio model sentences (teacher reads or uses recorded samples), whiteboard or shared doc, timer, notebook. Estimated level: 45 minutes total.

Lesson aims

  • Introduce and activate 8 high‑utility academic/abstract vocabulary items.
  • Practice accurate pronunciation for selected British English sounds and word stress patterns.
  • Build fluency through controlled and freer speaking tasks using new vocabulary and improved rhythm/intonation.

Target vocabulary (8 items)

Topic: Change, Responsibility and Decision‑making.

Teacher note: present each word with a short student‑friendly definition, one collocation, and one sentence. Use real examples related to the student’s interests or work.

Added vocabulary exercises

Exercise 1 — Matching and gap-fill (5 minutes)

Matching: Match A → B (write or say):

A: accountability; implement; feasible; stakeholder; anticipate; constraint; revise; robust

B: 1. limitation or restriction; 2. to put into action; 3. strong across conditions; 4. possible to do; 5. person with an interest; 6. responsibility for outcomes; 7. expect or predict; 8. change to improve.

Task: Say each matched pair aloud, then create a short sentence using the pair (30–40 seconds per word).

Exercise 2 — Collocation sorting (4 minutes)

Task: Put the collocations into two groups: ACTIONS and PEOPLE/CONDITIONS.

Collocations: take accountability; implement a policy; time constraints; consult stakeholders; revise the proposal; robust solution; anticipate problems; senior accountability.

Speak: For each collocation, make a 10–12 second comment relating it to a real situation (teacher prompts for context if needed).

Exercise 3 — Sentence transformation (6 minutes)

Task: Transform the sentences using the target word given in brackets; say the new sentence aloud.

  1. "We changed the plan because of the budget." (constraint) → "Because of budget constraints, we revised the plan."
  2. "They made sure someone was responsible." (accountability) → student transforms.
    3. 3. "We will put the new timetable into operation next week." (implement) → student transforms.

Teacher listens for natural stress and correct word form; correct and model where necessary.

Exercise 4 — Vocabulary active recall (homework-friendly)

Task: Choose 4 words and create a 90‑second spoken explanation for each (definition, one collocation, one example from your life). Record and self-evaluate for clarity and correct stress.

Pronunciation practice activities

Activity A — Focused phoneme drills (8 minutes)

Targets: /ɪ/ vs /iː/; weak forms (to /tə/), and multisyllabic stress patterns.

  1. Minimal pairs: ship /ʃɪp/ vs sheep /ʃiːp/; implement /ˈɪm.pl Log in or sign up to view See posts, photos and more on Facebook. m.pl ɪ.mənt/ vs implement in quick speech. Teacher models, student repeats in sets of 6, then uses in short phrases.
  2. Weak form drill: practice function words in short sentences (e.g., "We need to implement it" → "we need tə implement it"); student reduces and times themselves (aim for natural speed without losing clarity).
  3. Stress tap: for each multisyllabic target word, student taps the table on the stressed syllable then says the word (e.g., ac‑count‑A‑bil‑i‑ty — tap on 'BIL').

Activity B — Chunking and rhythm practice (5 minutes)

Task: Take two model sentences from the lesson and practise them in chunks. Example:

"We need to implement the plan / despite the budget constraints." — Pause at the slash, mark content words, practise stress and then say full sentence in natural rhythm 5 times.

Teacher models slowed, natural, and fast versions; student mirrors each.

Activity C — Intonation mapping (5 minutes)

Task: Use three short question and statement pairs and map intonation (rise/fall). Student reads the statement, then turns it into a question using rising intonation where appropriate.

Statement: "The revision is feasible." Question: "The revision is feasible?" (rising unless seeking confirmation — teacher explains pragmatic differences).

Record one example and play back; focus on natural pitch movement and final syllable lengthening for emphasis.

Activity D — Shadowing (homework / last 5 minutes)

Task: Teacher reads a 60–90 second passage containing 6 target words at natural pace. Student listens once, then immediately repeats (shadows) while trying to match rhythm, stress, and intonation. Repeat twice more, increasing speed slightly each time.

If recording tools are available, student records the third attempt and compares to the teacher model focusing on stress patterns and vowel quality.

Aim for short, frequent practice (5–10 minutes daily). Use recordings to self-monitor and focus on one pronunciation target per practice session.

Controlled practice (Pair/Teacher‑student) — 12 minutes

Activity A: Matching & sentence completion (5 min)

  1. Provide mixed definitions and collocations; student matches 8 words to meanings and completes sentence stems aloud (teacher times 3–4 minutes).
  2. Example stems: "Because of budget , we must the proposal." Student: "Because of budget constraints, we must revise the proposal."

Activity B: Role play — Decision meeting (7 min)

  1. Scenario: Student is a project lead presenting a revised plan to a stakeholder (teacher plays stakeholder). Use at least 5 target words and show natural sentence stress when emphasising constraints, feasibility and accountability.
  2. Prompt for student: "Present the plan changes, explain why the revision is feasible, and outline who has accountability." (3 minutes presentation; 4 minutes Q&A)

Encourage the student to use contractions and weak forms in replies (e.g., "we're", "we'll", "we'd") to sound more natural in connected speech.

Feedback & error correction — 6 minutes

  • Briefly note 3 strengths and 2 target corrections (one pronunciation, one vocabulary usage). Use the sandwich method: praise → correction → praise.
  • Pronunciation corrections: demonstrate corrected model, ask student to mirror twice, then place the corrected sentence back in the role‑play context.
  • Vocabulary correction: give a precise alternative if a wrong word was used (e.g., student said "responsible" but "accountability" needed); practice the corrected phrase twice.

Focus on intelligibility and natural rhythm rather than perfect accent. Small, repeatable changes give the best progress within one lesson.

Consolidation & homework

  • Immediate consolidation (in class): student writes 3 short written sentences using 5 of the target words and reads them aloud; teacher corrects stress and word choice.
  • Homework (micro‑tasks):
    • Record 2 minutes of the student summarising the role play; next lesson compare with today's recording and note improvements.
    • Write a short paragraph (80–120 words) describing a challenging decision and include at least 6 of the target words.
    • Daily practice: 5 minutes minimal pairs; 5 minutes stress tapping or shadowing one short passage.

Teacher script & quick prompts

  • Warm-up prompts: "Tell me about a change you experienced recently. Who took responsibility?"
  • Presentation prompts: "How would you paraphrase 'accountability' in one sentence?" "Give me a collocation for 'constraint'."
  • Pronunciation prompts: "Listen and repeat: /əˌkaʊn.təˈbɪl.ə.ti/ — now faster, with natural stress." "Which syllable is stressed in 'implement'?"
  • Role play prompt card (give to student): "You must convince the stakeholder that the revised plan is feasible despite constraints. Use accountability, implement, revise, and robust."
Notes: Adjust timings depending on student speed. Use personalised examples linked to the student's work/life to maximise retention. Aim for frequent short modelling and immediate repetition for pronunciation gains.
